# Proto-Personas

## Alex Rivera – Security-Conscious Solo Hobbyist / Home Power User
**Context**: Runs SecureClaw on a personal laptop or home server (Nvidia Jetson / old PC / Mac). Uses it nightly for personal automation (finance tracking, home lab, creative coding). Works day-job at a Tier-1 bank so demands the same controls at home that the bank would require.  
**Primary Goal**: Full autonomy + fun self-evolution without ever compromising trust — “I want the magic of OpenClaw but with the paranoia of my bank’s production systems.”  
**Biggest Pain**: Existing agent tools feel either too loose (scary at 2 a.m.) or too enterprise-heavy (clunky for one person).  
**Success Metric with SecureClaw**: Every change shows the full governance court in <30 seconds; Alex feels they personally “signed off” like a CISO would; sleep-at-night confidence is 100 %.  
**Product Needs**: Instant “About Me” onboarding, simulated persona-agents that debate in plain English, deferral options (“review in 24 h”), and NASA-style board with one-click approve/reject.

## Jordan Hale – Indie Developer / Small-Team Founder
**Context**: Bootstrapping a startup or open-source project; runs SecureClaw locally or on a cheap VPS; may later pitch to investors or enterprise clients.  
**Primary Goal**: Rapid iteration and self-improving agents while being able to export “enterprise-ready” audit trails and certified snapshots the moment they need to show diligence.  
**Biggest Pain**: Can’t afford a full security team, yet knows VCs and early customers will ask the same questions a bank CISO would.  
**Success Metric with SecureClaw**: The governance court automatically generates investor-grade reports (“Here’s why we approved this skill upgrade”); Jordan can toggle “simulate full bank review” mode for client demos.  
**Product Needs**: Profile-driven strictness sliders, exportable tamper-proof logs, and the ability for the constitutional agent to “role-play” a full Architecture Review Board.

## Sam Chen – Mid-Market Tech Lead / Platform Engineer
**Context**: Leads a 10–50 person team at a fintech or regulated SaaS company; deploys SecureClaw in a private cloud or on-prem for internal agentic workflows.  
**Primary Goal**: Start with hobbyist-speed local installs for the team, then let the agent itself guide the evolution into production-grade zero-trust without hiring extra governance staff.  
**Biggest Pain**: Open-source agent tools create shadow IT risks; manual reviews kill velocity.  
**Success Metric with SecureClaw**: The All-Hands Court replaces weekly security meetings; every team member sees the same simulated reviewer perspectives; deployments move from “experimental” to “Tier-1 approved” in weeks instead of months.  
**Product Needs**: Multi-user “About Me” profiles per team member, shared governance history, and sub-agents that can be assigned permanent roles (e.g., permanent “MRM Agent” reviewer).
